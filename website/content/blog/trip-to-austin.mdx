A two-day trip to Austin for Hasnain's birthday. I'd been to Austin briefly before, but this was the first time I actually got to explore it.

## Day 1

Got picked up at 4pm from College Station and drove down with Hasnain, my birthday buddy. On the ride we caught up and jammed to some music — his taste wasn't really my style, but I'm not the type to complain. It's not my car. Basic decency.

Once we got to Austin, we met up with Daniyal — a chill guy, CS major but also into philosophy. As someone with a weird self-taught philosophy of my own, I got interested in him immediately and started asking about his views. Talking philosophy is always a lot of fun. We clearly didn't agree on everything, but I respected where he was coming from.

### Victory Lap and the UFC card

After that we hit up **Victory Lap**, a sports bar — only the second bar I've ever been to. We went mainly to catch the World Cup match, Argentina vs. Sweden. I've never really cared about the World Cup, but it wasn't my birthday, so not my call. They were also showing UFC, which I was way more into. Royval vs. Kavanagh was an amazing fight — close and packed with action. The rest of the card was kind of disappointing, though. The main event, McGregor vs. Holloway after his five-year hiatus, was the one I'd been waiting for — and it fell flat. McGregor TKO'd himself in under two minutes. Wild.

### Food and Sixth Street

After the fights we grabbed food from a burger truck. My birthday buddy and the other friend liked it, but I thought it was worse than Whataburger for the price.

Then we checked out Sixth Street, which was rough. An actual city of sin — people dressed skimpy everywhere, smoke from cigs and vapes, alcohol, and music so loud I could feel it in my stomach. We tried a bar called the Aquarium, which was more like a sardine can — so packed you couldn't move without bumping into someone. Hasnain and I bailed in under 20 minutes; it was a first for both of us. The only bar I'd been to before was Harry's in College Station, a dance bar built around Texas two-step. The Aquarium gave me a whole new appreciation for Harry's, which honestly feels a lot classier — and that's coming from someone who doesn't drink and generally isn't into nightlife or bar culture.

We called it a night after that. I crashed on Andrii's couch — the view from his apartment was great, even if the place itself was a little cold. Overall a good time.

## Day 2

Started slow. I always struggle to sleep the first night somewhere new, so I didn't crash until around 3am and woke up around 11. Spent the morning just playing PlayStation with Andrii — genuinely fun, even though I'm not really a console gamer.

![Andrii on the couch](/blog/trip-to-austin/andrii-couch.jpg)

### Sushi, the library, and bouldering

We got lunch with Hasnain at **Ra Sushi**. I'm always skeptical of sushi places because College Station has a sushi buffet called Sushi Masa that's decent quality for the price, so anywhere else has to be at least twice as good to justify the cost. Most places don't clear that bar — Ra Sushi actually did. Quality was surprisingly high.

Next was the Austin Public Library to get some work done. On the surface, it's impressive — huge, tons of facilities, even a board game collection, way bigger than anything in College Station. But the reality is a little different: during the day, if you look past the surface, a lot of it feels more like a homeless shelter than a library. Get close to where people are gathered and there are spots that reek of BO, and most of the computers are occupied by someone in ragged clothes with their bags leaned up against the desk.

We met back up with Daniyal to go bouldering at Austin Bouldering Project. Got stuck in the library parking garage for a solid 30 minutes first, unfortunately. The gym itself was awesome — again, way bigger than Stone Co, our local spot in College Station. Would've loved to stay longer, but we had to head out after a while.

Grabbed food at P. Terry's on the way — worse than Whataburger again, but a lot cheaper. Then dropped everyone off and headed back to College Station.

## Austin takeaways

Austin is a much bigger city than College Station, but overall I'd still rate College Station higher.

**The pros:** bigger, more food options, more to do.

**The cons:**

1. **Homelessness.** We just don't have this in College Station, and it hits quality of life hard for someone who walks a lot in cities. It's an attack on multiple senses — sight (piles of belongings you can't distinguish from trash), smell, and a kind of sixth sense where you're never fully at ease, not knowing who around you might be unstable.
2. **Inconsistent architecture.** This one's a bit double-edged — it makes walking around genuinely interesting, since every block is something different, from a historic building to a skyscraper. But it also threw me off and felt unnatural, maybe just because I've lived in College Station my whole life and I'm not used to it. The uneven sidewalks didn't help either — if I wasn't watching my step, I could've easily tripped.

Even with all that, I've still got unfinished business in Austin, so I'll probably go back. Top of the list is the food — a lot of recommendations I never got to try. Austin has three Michelin-starred restaurants and I haven't been to a single one. Cabo Bob's was recommended by multiple people, but it's closed on Sundays, so that didn't happen either. I also want to check out the Domain — I've seen it all over livestreams and just want to see it in person, even if there's not much for me to actually do there.
